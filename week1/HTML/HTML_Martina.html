Mallard Ducks

Mallard ducks (Anas platyrhynchos) are one of the most common and recognizable duck species in the world. They are widely found in wetlands, parks, ponds, rivers, and lakes.

Characteristics

  • Male: Bright green head, white neck ring, and chestnut-brown chest.
  • Female: Mottled brown plumage for camouflage.
  • Both males and females have distinctive blue speculum feathers on their wings.

Fun Fact

Did you know? Mallard ducks are dabbling ducks, meaning they feed by tipping forward in the water rather than diving.

Habitat

Mallards are found in a variety of habitats, including:

  1. Freshwater lakes and rivers
  2. Urban parks
  3. Wetlands and marshes
